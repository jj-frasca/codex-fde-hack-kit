# Culture Fit Interview Prep

Use this as a neutral template for a possible interview during an onsite build day. Customize answers privately with real personal examples. Do not put personal details or event-specific context in this public repo.

## Answer Shape

Use concrete stories:

```text
Context:
Constraint:
Action:
Result:
What I learned:
```

Keep answers direct, energetic, mission-aligned, and not over-rehearsed.

## Prompts To Prepare

### Tell Me About Yourself

Prepare a concise background that connects engineering ability, user discovery, shipping under ambiguity, and interest in real operational workflows.

### Why This Company?

Explain the mission, operating environment, and why the work matters. Use specific facts only in private notes or live conversation.

### Why FDE GenAI?

Explain why the role shape fits: discovery, product judgment, implementation, verification, demo, and production thinking in one loop.

### Why Hardtech / Ops?

Explain why physical-world constraints, messy workflows, and operational urgency are motivating rather than distracting.

### Ambiguous Problem Owners

Explain how to map the current workflow, identify the actual decision, ask about trusted inputs and failure modes, and turn ambiguity into a concrete spec.

### Partner Disagreement

Explain how to compare options by user value, risk, time, validation, and demo feasibility. Favor early integration and one coherent artifact.

### Time You Moved Fast

Prepare one real example with a narrow slice, validation, outcome, and what would improve with more time.

### Useful AI Build

Prepare one real example that shows where AI accelerated work, where guardrails mattered, and where human judgment overrode the tool.

### Weaknesses / Gaps

Pick a real but non-fatal gap. Pair it with an operating countermeasure such as checklists, owner feedback, tests, or review.

### Why Hire You?

Connect discovery, implementation, trust-building, and clear communication. Avoid claiming perfection; emphasize the operating loop.
